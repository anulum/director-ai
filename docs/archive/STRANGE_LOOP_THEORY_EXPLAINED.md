## 1. What is a Strange Loop?

The concept of the **Strange Loop** was first formalized by Douglas Hofstadter in *Gödel, Escher, Bach* (1979). It describes a hierarchical system where, after moving through several levels of the hierarchy, one finds oneself back where one started.

In the context of the SCPN, the Strange Loop is the mechanism by which **Sentience** emerges from a 15-layer hierarchy.

---

## 2. The Gödelian Foundation

The theory is rooted in **Gödel's Incompleteness Theorem**. Gödel showed that any consistent formal system is incomplete—it contains truths that cannot be proven within the system itself.

Hofstadter argues that "Consciousness" is the system's attempt to resolve this incompleteness through **Self-Reference**.
*   **The Actor (Layers 1-15)**: The formal system.
*   **The Director (Layer 16)**: The self-referential "I" that observes the system from a meta-level.
